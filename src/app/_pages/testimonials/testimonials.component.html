Chris Hollis

Coach Kevin Mills, Has been a mentor and father figure to me since I was about 16 years old. He was my Head Football Coach at Pasadena High School. Coach Kevin Mills helped push me to understand how to overcome adversity, and accept new challenges in life. Also how to get through tough circumstances without doubting myself.

Mills instilled in me that tough spirit of being a determined and motivated individual and not being susceptible to having the mentality of a victim and feeling like the world owes me something. Mills always told me that having an impact requires sacrifice and that loyalty is everything. Without coach Kevin Mills my life trajectory would have been very different due to my environment growing up as a young black man in my neighborhood.

Due to Coach mills mentor-ship of me, i continued my football and academic career in college at Mount San Antonio College. Where I graduated with an Associates Degree in Kinesiology and Wellness, and made the deans list for my academic achievements. From there i went on to Azusa Pacific University where i continued my football and academic career, and graduated with a Bachelors Degree in Sociology. While being a intern for a well known business organization called "Enactus", where i held the position off discussion facilitator. I am currently working in the area of community development in Los Angeles County. I serve as a Board Member as well for a non-profit organization called "ImPower Youth" at the age of twenty-six years old. Along with all of that i am continuing my education in pursuits of an M.B.A degree from University's such as, USC, Howard University, Georgetown, Chicago Booth School of Business, and a few more. I say all this to conclude that without Coach Kevin Mills in my life none of this would be possible at all. he instilled in me the tools at a critical age in my life to succeed and overcome everything that life has thrown at me.

Dylan Robinson

I have had the pleasure of being the both a colleague and a client of Mr. Kevin Mills. I first met Kevin when I began teaching English at Pasadena High School where he was Head Varsity Football Coach at the time. Kevin’s stellar reputation preceded he and I actually meeting face to face. We had several friends in common and our fellow teachers thought the world of him. Kevin’s players were the most respectable and well behaved of all the athletes on campus. I can honestly say I never had to write a referral or call the parent of a footballer because the buck stopped with Kevin. As much as Kevin is a steadfast disciplinarian, he was also their confidant; a balance that most coaches are never able to achieve.

Over the years Kevin and I have built a friendship and eventually I contracted with Kevin to tutor my son, Dylan in Math. Which soon turned into Kevin tutoring my son in anything and everything. He quickly and effortlessly became my son’s mentor. Based on Kevin’s background and his strong relationship with my son he was the first person I called when Dylan began to struggle with keeping his temper while competing in soccer games. My son is hard on himself on top of being one of a few African-American young men that play soccer locally. He is often faced with adversity through constant taunting and sometimes flat out prejudice. Because Kevin had experience in raising an African-American professional soccer player and is a former athlete himself, I knew he had something to offer Dylan on what he was currently facing. After just one meeting I saw a difference. While Dylan is still an intense player he, over time, has learned how to harness his energy and to power though the adversity he faces on the field without reacting to every attack.

The two continue to speak pretty regularly and Kevin remains apart of village helps me keeps Dylan’s eye on the prize. As a single mother I can only do and say so much. I am fortunate to have place to send my son to get support and guidance from a masculine point of view and I can trust. I cannot put a monetary value on what Kevin has provided my son. It has proved priceless.
